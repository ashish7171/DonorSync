Eligibility Criteria

Safe Donations and Superior Care

Our blood donation process is stringent and ensures the highest standards of safety and care. Whether you are a first-time donor or a regular, our comprehensive eligibility guidelines are designed to protect your health and the recipients who depend on these vital donations.

Age and Weight Requirements

Donors must be at least 18 years old in most states, with no upper age limit as long as they have good health. Additionally, donors should weigh at least 45 kg and meet the minimum hemoglobin level requirements. This ensures that the donation process is safe for the donor and that the donated blood is viable for use.

General Health

Individuals should be in good general health and feeling well on the day of donation. Donors must not be suffering from any infectious diseases or be on antibiotics. A brief health and lifestyle questionnaire must be completed to screen for conditions that might disqualify a potential donor, ensuring the safety of both the donor and the recipient.

Medication and Travel History

Potential donors must disclose any medications they are taking as some may disqualify them from donating. Additionally, travel history is important because visiting certain countries can expose individuals to diseases that might be transmitted through blood, such as malaria or Zika virus, leading to a temporary deferral.
